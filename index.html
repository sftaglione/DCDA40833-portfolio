Semester Reflection

What I learned: In this lab, I learned how to critically analyze a data visualization instead of just looking at it and accepting it as true. Using Tufte’s framework made me realize how much context, detail, and explanation matter when presenting data. I also learned how strong headlines and design choices can influence how people interpret information.

Challenges I overcame: One challenge I faced was moving beyond simply describing the infographic and actually analyzing it. At first, I kept explaining what I saw instead of evaluating how effective it was. I worked through this by re-reading the framework and asking myself deeper questions about what was missing and what assumptions the graphic might be encouraging.

How I grew: This lab helped me think more critically about visuals I see online, especially when they make bold claims. I now pay more attention to where data comes from, what context is missing, and whether conclusions are fully supported. It made me more aware of how design and argument connect.

Looking ahead: As the semester continues, I want to keep improving my ability to balance design and accuracy in my own work. I also want to get more comfortable questioning data instead of just accepting it at face value.
