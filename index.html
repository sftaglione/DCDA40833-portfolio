Semester Reflection

What I learned: Throughout this portfolio project, I learned how different digital tools can work together to analyze and present information. Across the labs we used several different platforms, including AI tools, Tableau, and Python, and each one helped me understand a different part of the digital analysis process. I learned how to evaluate AI outputs critically, analyze data visualizations, explore patterns in datasets, and use coding tools to turn information into something interactive and visual.

Challenges I overcame: One challenge I faced was becoming comfortable with tools that were new to me, especially Tableau and some of the coding tasks. At first it was easy to focus on simply completing the assignment instead of understanding how the tools actually worked. As I worked through each lab, I spent more time testing things, reviewing outputs, and troubleshooting small problems. That process helped me understand the logic behind the tools rather than just following instructions.

How I grew: Over the semester I became much more confident working with digital tools and interpreting data. Instead of feeling intimidated by new platforms, I started approaching them with curiosity and experimentation. I also became more aware of how design, data, and technology all influence how information is communicated and interpreted.

Looking ahead: Moving forward, I want to keep building on these skills by becoming more comfortable combining coding, data analysis, and communication. The labs showed me how technical tools can support storytelling and analysis, and I want to continue developing those skills in future projects and courses.
